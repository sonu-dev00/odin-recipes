garlic mushroom cauliflower skillet

This garlic mushroom cauliflower skillet is an elegant one-pan side dish. Sautéed mushrooms and cauliflower in garlic butter finish under the broiler with a golden panko-Parmesan crust and a hint of fresh thyme and lemon.

Ingrediants

  • 2 tablespoons butter. melted, divided
  • 2 cups frozen mushrooms, such as BetterGoods, thawed and drained
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced or more to taste
  • 1 small head caulitflower, cut into small florets
  • salt and freshly ground black peper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/3 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice, to finish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven’s broiler to High and set a rack about 6 inches from the heat source.
  2. Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until they release their liquid and start to brown, 5 to 7 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute more.
  3. Stir in cauliflower florets; season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Cook until cauliflower is slightly tender, 5 to 6 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. In a small bowl, mix panko with Parmesan, fresh thyme, and remaining melted butter. Sprinkle evenly over the cauliflower-mushroom mixture in the skillet.
  5. Place the skillet under the broiler, watching closely, until panko is golden brown and crispy, 3 to 5 minutes.
  6. Remove from the oven and drizzle with lemon juice for brightness. Serve immediately.
